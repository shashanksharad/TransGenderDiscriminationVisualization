A Potrait of Transgenders

A Data Visualization exercise to remind modern society of the repercussions of transgender discrimination through history

Created by Sharad Shashank

Prologue

This data visualization exercise about transgender discrimination is both saddening & hopeful at the same time. Anybody reading this has my humble request to take a moment to think and realize that we belong to the same social setup that has historically been very unjust, discriminatory and often violent to the transgender community and thus all of us owe our contributions towards creating a better world for them to live.

Transgender Discrimination

Transgender people have been neglected throughout the world for a long time. Except for few countries, there is no recognition of their rights and they are bound to live very miserable lives with widespread discrimination in social, political, economic and educational spheres. In Indian society where I come from, this sexual minority has predominantly been forced into begging or prostitution. As a kid I was taught to despise them and have prejudices without any scientific reason. They have faced social and economic alienation due to discrimination based on their gender identity or expression. 
Lack of sufficient data on their discrimination has further hampered substantial improvement in transgender equality through government policies. Through this work, I've tried to compile & visualize some transgender discrimination survey data and thus highlight the extent of wrongdoings that they have to go through just because of how nature wanted them to be born as.

How the World supports Transgenders
From a survey of 23 countries, Spain ranks highest in support for Transgender rights while Russia ranks lowest. Hover on each country to know their scores(0-100).
Starting on a positive note
Majorities in all 23 countries believe that transgender people deserve the right to change their identity documents to be consistent with their gender identity, though a substantial portion believes that some form of regulation from medical professionals, medical interventions, and/or government is necessary.
Inequality Bubbles
Transgender people face severe discrimination in almost all spheres of social life, especially in Education, Employment, Healthcare & Public accommodation. Each bubble here represents a discrimination that transgenders in the US faced where color represents discrimination category and size represents percentage of transgender population affected. Hover for details.
The Dominant ones
Out of the many inequality fronts, majority of the transgender population have been impacted these six issues.
Race
The distribution of race amongst the respondents is dominated by white people. This demographic and sociographic distribution could be underrepresenting some races because of the survey's sample bias.
Age
The difference in age distribution between transgender and general population is representing differing understanding and social acceptance of gender expression/identity among different generations.
Household Income
The respondents reported living in extreme poverty. This statistics shows that trans people are four times more likely to have a household income less that $10k per year compared to the general population.
Household Income by Gender Identity
Although household income varies between men and women in the US, it surprisingly doesn't depend on transgender people's gender identity or expression.
Employment Disparity
70% of transgender people in the US are currently employed, 11% are unemployed. Infact the unemployment rate tends to be 14%, twice that of the general population (7%).
Educational Attainment
Compared to other measures of economic well being where trans people fare much worse than the general population, they havea surprisingly higher level of education attainment!
Sexual Orientation
The sexuality of transgenders demonstrates a diverse spectrum. This wide distribution speaks to the complexity of sexual orientation for those whose gender identity or expression may have changed during their lives.
